The Problem

Existing QDAS - Unintuitive clunky and frustrating

More about the problem  

During our time as researchers, we noticed that some QDAS are feature-rich but highly unintuitive, often requiring days or weeks just to get started (much less do anything). Others are more intuitive but so clunky and lacking in features that they’re unusable. Many of the researchers that we interviewed said they ended up doing analyses by hand out of sheer frustration with all QDAS!

The Solution

Simple to use QDAS, access anywhere and anytime, ability to collaborate with others and a plethora of powerful tools and features!

More about the solution  

After conducting interviews and focus groups with students and early career researchers to find out what was most important to them, we found the following common concerns:


Access anywhere and anytime

All QDAS on the market require an installation of some kind. Ours requires absolutely nothing other than a browser. This means you can access your projects from any machine with an internet connection.


Sharing data / projects with collaborators

Many of our interviewees expressed concerns about not being able to share data due to versioning. Our application has no versions, no update dialogs, and makes use of state-of-the-art databases, so sharing is a non-issue.


Simple and easy to use

No more endless clicking and waiting for pages to load!

By far the most voiced concern, interviewees were frustrated by functionality and simple tasks being spread out all over the application, which can make iterative and/or hermeneutic approaches to coding a nightmare of clicking and screen loading.

Our application has a clear step-by-step process of project creation, uploading of media & descriptor creation, coding, analyses and more. Each step also contains everything you need for that process on one screen as a single page application. This keeps students and ECRs on track!

Power and Features

Code Suggestions, Data Visualisation, Auto Analysis, Reporting & Finding Collaborators

More about power and features  

One of the more interesting requests that came from senior-level students and early career researchers was for the QDAS to have some way to suggest high-level / meta codes based on what had been coded up along with the researcher’s theoretical lens and/or specific methodology. We agreed, and this will actually be a module in itself.

Data visualisation was another top concern, and many expressed the limited nature of current QDAS. So we are including a plethora of interactive and animated data visualization tools. While we cannot reveal the exact number of these yet, rest assured you will be blown away.

Our QDAS will include most, if not all, of the auto analyses that you would expect to see in other QDAS (e.g. content analysis, word stems/trees, code clouds, etc.). However, we won’t stop at that and will be including many, many more, with our ongoing aim to release at least one major feature per quarter once launched!

Reporting for supervisors and journal editors/reviewers was also a clear concern, and so our application will including reporting features such as the exact time and date that codes were applied to excerpts, merged with other codes, and other data, so that the entire process (including abstraction) can be easily reconstructed. This means that reconstruction concerns are alleviated and you can focus on coding and analyzing.


Finding Collaborators

Getting you to the right people

This was actually our idea but it was considered by far the most exciting by interviewees. We all know (or if you are early enough, will know) the institutional politics that often keep novice researchers from finding the right collaborators.

While many have expressed concerns with how we might use data, we are being absolutely upfront and continuously transparent about how we will use your data - we will use it to get you to the right people, period . This is our passion and our core belief. Furthermore, many senior academics will tell you that language can make or break just about any paper or thesis. Hence, much of our efforts will be matching collaborators based on language, whether it be codes used, the texts themselves or literature based on researcher background/lens.

Apart from providing you with the most helpful and state-of-the-art QDAS on the market, planned features include:

And, of course, continuous improvements to the social page that’ll help you find like-minded collaborators!
